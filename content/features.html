Web services

Lelylan is a platform based on a set if REST web services that allows the access to all home automation resources (devices, locations, people and more). What we aim to do is mapping every device to a unique URI which will provide control over it. When such a connection is done, technological problems disappear and devices become easily identifiable through their functionalities and nothing more. Learn more about Lelylan API →

Unite Nodes

Unite Nodes are gateway that allow Lelylan to communicate with a set of devices based on a specific standard or protocol. When devices are connected through a Unite Node, they are physically accessible from all services built on top of Lelylan and they can communicate to each other without the technological limitations we face today.

To make this possible we are working hard, covering standards like Zigbee, Konnex, Lonworks, X10, Z-Wave and lot more. We also love to hack and prototype new stuff everyday with solutions like Arduino and ShevaPlug. Learn more about Unite Nodes →

Mashups & Services

The integrations with Lelylan are endless and they covers lot of fields, like energy saving, security and user experience. Here some example of what we can define.

  • Rule engines - close the cooling system if the window in the same room is opened
  • Scenery systems - soft light could be the scenery, turning the intensity of lights down
  • Recommendation systems - suggest to use appliances when prices are cheaper
  • Scheduling systems open the shutters in the bedroom every working day at 7am.

Innovation

With Lelylan there is a new approach to the market, where we give everybody the ability to create new solutions. With Lelylan you do not start from the physical domotics systems. You start from a fake domotic system that anyone can create and play with and only when you feel satisfied you can connect to the physical world.

As final result we imagine you going to the IKEA or to the shop close to your house, buying a light or any other device, going back home, plugging it and getting it available on web, trough an URI. We envision a system that everybody can use, something simple to use and fast to install. The device will then be able to talk with the other devices in the house, becoming smarter and following your needs in a better way.
